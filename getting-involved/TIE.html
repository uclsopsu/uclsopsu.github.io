In Western societies we are now living in a data exhaust that is growing exponentially. In 2005 we had handled 130 Exabytes of data, which grew to 1200 Exabytes in 2010 and then 7900 Exabytes in 2015. Humans can analyse a fraction of this amount whilst machines can analyse just below double this amount. However, with machine learning (ML) we can make sense of and add value to these larger amounts of data. Comparatively, consider for a second that the Human Genome contains only around 725MB of data.



Why Should Pharmacy Students Care?

Because healthcare providers are increasingly using cognitive analytics with patient data to improve both diagnostics and predict outcomes. With regards to the former, IBM Watson (IBM's natural language processing computer system) has recently been taught to diagnose cancer. It used to take it a year to learn, whereas now it takes 1 month's worth of doctoral input. Regarding predicting outcomes, IBM are collaborating with Pfizer on an initiative called ''BlueSky'' where they are using remote monitoring solutions (ML, sensors, and electronic devices) for Parkinson's Disease to improve clinical trials.



The Three Strands

Technology

This strand will look at some of the emerging technologies used in pharmacy in the U.S and other technological hotspots. Particular attention will therefore be paid to using these ideas and improving them for the U.K marketplace. The medium for member’s engagement will be team round-table discussions, presentations, TED-style talks, and guest lectures with Q&A format.


The strand will serve as a medium for technologically-savvy pharmacy students to share their work, collaborate with others, and express their views. In this regard, all members will be encouraged to pursue online courses, particularly through LinkedIn, in areas relating to artificial intelligence, machine learning, and big data analytics.


It will also organise ‘alternative careers’ talks, liaising with pharmaceutical industries, etc. A potential upcoming collaboration with Google is also pending!



Innovation

This strand is the workhorse of the society. Members will actively set meetings to discuss ideas and concepts related to those discussed in the technology strand. However, particular attention will be paid to generating novel ideas – solving problems faced by doctors, pharmacists, and other healthcare professionals in their day-to-day practice.


There will be talks from lecturers from the School of Pharmacy, as well as external speakers on the concept of intellectual property and pharma start-ups. Our members will also be invited to attend workshops, and to take part in various Hackathons and competitions.



Enterprise

This strand will serve as the outreaching arm of the society. Its aim will be to reach out to other societies with similar goals and generate collaborative events such as competitions.
