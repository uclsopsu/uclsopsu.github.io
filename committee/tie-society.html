Founder & President: Blend Ashtey

MPharm Year 4 


I am a fourth-year at the UCL School of Pharmacy with a passion for effecting change in the context of health and disease. I will soon be concentrating on my research project in pharmaceutics (the design and manufacture of medicines), whilst also networking and developing ideas with healthcare technology start-ups and start-up accelerators.


Having interned in a variety of different settings, including not-for-profit organisations, community and hospital pharmacy, contract research organisations (CROs), and pharmaceutical industry I have recently undertaken the challenge of building a team centered on developing technological solutions to common problems faced in pharmaceutical and medical practice. This is in conjunction with producing written material (currently in progress) on the emergence of technology into healthcare, specifically in integrating tech culture into pharmacy, which I believe to be an unmet educational (and professional) need. My other writing projects include a guide on mathematics for pharmacists with a projected release date of 2019.


Through these experiences I have obtained an awareness of the need to anticipate change, act responsibly, and lead by example. My latest internship at AbbVie Inc. involved working on a cross-functional project designed to optimize business efficiencies, thereby broadening my scope for tackling problems in a non-scientific context. This better enables me to solve healthcare issues as the ability to make an impact on patients and patient organisations is becoming more difficult
