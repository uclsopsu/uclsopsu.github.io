What are they about?




The Academic Support Groups are here to augment the lectures and workshops you will be having. The aim of these groups are to quickly introduce you to the overall academic year structure, examinations format and to supply you with academic resources to help you reach your goal academically.


The special element of these support groups is that they are held by students in upper years that have been through the course and survived, and will thus be some of the best people to give you the necessary help you require to make you successful in the exams and degree as a whole.


The groups are for students in year 1 to 3 in their MPharm degree. There will be biannual group meetings on each module (one at the half-term week and one in the last week of the first two semesters), as well an individual meetings with you support group mentor whenever you need.



How to get involved?

Become a Support Group Mentor

If you are an MPharm Year 2, 3 or 4 student who would like to take part in this exciting yet rewarding project, contact Emmanuel Mensah stating your year group and which module you will like to help with. You should also provide a short statement why you think you would be suited for the role. The modules are PHAY1001, PHAY1002, PHAY1003, PHAY1004, PHAY2001, PHAY2002, PHAY2003, PHAY2004, PHAY3101, PHAY3102, PHAY3103 and PHAY3104.


Student notes, teaching plans and past examination resources will be provided for you to help your mentees upon request.



Join An Academic Support Group

If you are an MPharm Year 1, 2 or 3 student who will like some student-based learning sessions, contact Emmanuel Mensah stating your year group. You and other students will be assigned to a mentor who will guide you through the year academically to ensure you are successful in the academic year. We will provide advice on learning methods, coursework and examination resources.



Notes and Exam Resources

In your time here at SoP, you will soon (or later) find out that due to the restructuring of the course, past examination papers are very scare to come by. However, these support groups have resources from both the old (only relevant parts!) and new course structure.
